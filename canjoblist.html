NEW new
Federal Skilled worker category is designed for individuals who wish to immigrate to Canada on the basis of their own credentials. To qualify for Skilled worker category, applicants will be assessed on different factors of Education, Age, Work Experience, Language proficiency, Arranged Employment and Adaptability factors. Under Adaptability factor applicants will get points for their Spouse education, Canadian education/ work and close relatives in Canada. To qualify under this category applicants needs to attain 67 points.
Calculating your points under Skilled worker category can be complicated. It is more than simply adding up the points. You need to have detail understanding of Canadian immigration law and procedures. It is recommended that you get a professional assessment to accurately calculate your score as per the latest rules and regulations of Canadian immigration.
Please note that to qualify under the Skilled Worker Category, you need to have at least one year of continuous full-time or equivalent part-time (37.5 hours per week or more) work experience within the past ten years in the occupations listed in either Skill Type 0 or Skill Level A or B of the National Occupational Classification (NOC).
If you have met with the requirements of one year work experience in occupations listed under Skill Type O, A or B, then applicants will be assessed according to various selection criteria. Applicants must attain a minimum score of sixty-seven (67) points overall in order to be eligible to become permanent residents of Canada.
